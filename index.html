Cupcakes in cups!


Cupcakes:










Chocolate Cupcake:

Ingredients quantaties
Self-raising flour 4 tbsp
Caster sugar 4 tbsp
Cocoa powder 2 tbsp
Medium egg 1
milk 3 tbsp
Vegetable oil 3 tbsp
Vanilla essence a few drops
Chocolate chips 2 tbsp

STEP 1

Mix the flour, sugar and cocoa power in a large mug.


STEP 2

Add the egg and mix well. (Don't worry if it's too dry)


STEP 3

Add the milk and oil aswell as the vanilla, and mix.


STEP 4

Microwave on high for 90 to 120 seconds, or until it stops rising.


Vanilla Sponge:

Ingredients quantaties
Unsalted butter 2 tbsp
Milk 1 tbsp
Vanilla 1/2 tsp
Egg yolk 1
White sugar 2 tbsp
All-purpose flour 4 tbsp
Baking powder 1/2 tsp
Spronkles 1 tbsp

STEP 1

Add the butter in a large mug and microwave until melted (~30 seconds).


STEP 2

Add the milk, vanilla, egg yolk and sugar, and mix well.


STEP 3

Add the flour and baking powder, and mix.


STEP 4

Fold in the spronkles.


STEP 5

Microwave on medium for 60 to 90 seconds, or until it stops rising.


STEP 6

Allow to sit for at least 1 minute.


Red Velvet:

Ingredients quantaties
Milk 3 tbsp
White vinegar 1/2 tsp
Flour 4 tbsp
Sugar 2 tbsp
Cocoa power 1 tsp
Baking power 1/4 tsp
Vegetable oil 3/2
Red food colouring 5 drops
Powdered sugar for dusting

STEP 1

Whisk together the milk and vinegar in a large mug.


STEP 2

In another bowl mix the all dry ingredients, Then add to the milk mixture and mix


STEP 3

Add the oil and food colouring and mix until smooth.


STEP 4

Microwave on high for 70 seconds, or until it stops rising. After dust with the powered sugar.


Blueberry:

Ingredients quantaties
All-purpose flour 4 tbsp
Baking power 1/4 tsp
Granulated sugar 1 tbsp
Milk 3 tbsp
Vegetable oil 1/2 tbsp
Vanilla 1/4 tsp
Blueberries 10

STEP 1

Mix together all the ingredients except the blueberries in a large mug.


STEP 2

Fold in the blueberries.


STEP 3

Microwave on high for 70 to 85 seconds, or until it stops rising.

Dame Mary Rosa Alleyne Hunnings DBE, known professionally as Mary Berry, is a British food writer, chef and television presenter. After being encouraged in domestic science classes at school, she studied catering and shipping management at college.

She has published more than 75 cookery books, including her best-selling Baking Bible in 2009. Her first book was The Hamlyn All Colour Cookbook in 1970. She hosted several television series for the BBC and Thames Television. Berry is an occasional contributor to Woman's Hour and Saturday Kitchen. She was a judge on the BBC One (originally BBC Two) television programme The Great British Bake Off from its launch in 2010 until 2016, when it relocated to Channel 4
